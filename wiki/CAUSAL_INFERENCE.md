# Causal Inference & Impact Analysis

Understanding causality, not just correlation.

---

## 🔗 Correlation vs Causation

**Correlation**: X and Y move together
- Might be causal
- Might be coincidence
- Might be confounding variable

**Causation**: X causes Y
- Stronger claim
- Requires evidence
- More actionable

---

## 🎯 Causal Questions

"Does marketing increase sales?" (causal)
vs.
"Do high-sales companies market more?" (correlation)

---

## 🔬 Causal Methods

**Randomized Experiments**: Gold standard
- Random assignment eliminates confounding
- Expensive and slow

**Observational Methods**:
- Matching: Find similar untreated units
- Instrumental Variables: Use natural experiments
- Difference-in-Differences: Pre/post comparison

---

## 📊 Causal Graphs

```
Marketing → Sales
       ↓
   ↙ Budget ↙
(confounding)
```

Unconfounded: Marketing → Sales
Confounded: Budget affects both

---

## 💡 Applications

- Marketing effectiveness
- Treatment impact (medical)
- Policy evaluation
- Product feature impact
- Price elasticity
